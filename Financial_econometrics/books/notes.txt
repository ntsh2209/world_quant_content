This is a quant developer/strat role focused on derivatives pricing and risk management in their Synthetic Products Group. Here's your comprehensive prep plan:

1. Core Mathematical Foundations
Stochastic Calculus & Probability
Brownian Motion: properties, quadratic variation, martingale properties
Itô's Lemma: derivation and applications to option pricing
Stochastic Differential Equations (SDEs): geometric Brownian motion for stock prices
Martingales & Risk-neutral pricing: fundamental theorems of asset pricing
Girsanov Theorem: change of measure (for quanto products, FX hedging)
Resources:
Shreve's Stochastic Calculus for Finance II (Chapters 1-5)
Hull's Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives (Chapters 13-15, 26)

2. Derivatives Pricing Models
Equity Derivatives (Critical)
Black-Scholes Model: assumptions, derivation, Greeks
Equity Swaps: pricing total return swaps, dividend treatment, funding spreads
Variance Swaps: volatility products, convexity adjustments
Exotic Options: barriers, digitals, autocallables
Local Volatility Models: Dupire's formula, calibration to market skew
Stochastic Volatility: Heston model basics
Futures & Forwards
Cost of carry model: contango vs backwardation
Dividend adjustments for equity futures
Rolling strategies and basis risk
Resources:
Hull (Chapters 7-10, 16-18, 27)
Wilmott on Quantitative Finance (Vol 2: Exotic Options)
Papers: Read about equity swap mechanics (ISDA documentation overview)

3. Risk Management
Greeks & Hedging
Delta hedging: dynamic replication, P&L explain
Gamma risk: managing convexity, gamma scalping
Vega hedging: volatility surface risk
Cross-gamma (FX/Equity): quanto products
One Delta products: likely refers to delta-one (futures, swaps, forwards)
Backtesting
VaR models: historical, parametric, Monte Carlo
P&L attribution: decomposing trading desk P&L
Hedging effectiveness: analyzing slippage, transaction costs
Stress testing: scenario analysis for tail risk
Resources:
Hull (Chapter 23: Credit Risk, Chapter 24: Credit Derivatives)
Jorion's Value at Risk (for risk metrics)

Data Structures & Optimization
Efficient portfolio representations (hash maps for positions)
Time series analysis for backtesting
